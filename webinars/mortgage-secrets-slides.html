Section 1 — The Real Problem + The Offset Fix

The Number Nobody Shows You —
And How to Fight It From Day One

Script
"I want to start by showing you something that shocks almost every single person I show it to.

Let's say you have a $500,000 mortgage. You faithfully make your payment every single month. You're doing everything right.

But here's what's actually happening with that payment.

In your very first year — for every single dollar you send to the bank — 83 cents goes straight to the bank as interest. Only 17 cents actually reduces what you owe on your home.

Let that sink in for a moment.

You're essentially renting your own house from the bank for the first 12 years of your mortgage.

The bank designed it this way on purpose. It's called amortization — and it's the single most expensive financial reality most Canadians never fully understand.

By the time you're done paying off a $500,000 mortgage over 25 years — you will have paid the bank over $400,000 in interest alone.

Now here's the thing — none of that is inevitable.

And the first strategy I want to show you is called the Offset Mortgage — and it attacks that interest number directly, starting on day one.

Here's how it works in plain language. With an offset mortgage, your savings account is linked directly to your mortgage balance. The bank only charges you interest on the difference.

So if you have a $500,000 mortgage and $40,000 sitting in savings — you only pay interest on $460,000. That $40,000 is working for you 24 hours a day, 7 days a week — without you doing anything differently.

No extra payments. No locked-in GICs. Your money is still yours — you can access it anytime. But every dollar sitting there is silently cutting your interest bill every single day."

Problem 3 — Rental Property Owners

Your Rental Property Should Be Working Harder For You

Script
"If you own a rental property and a personal home with a mortgage, what I'm about to share might be the most valuable 5 minutes of your entire year.

Most rental property owners are paying far more tax than they legally need to.

Here's what a small number of very smart Canadian property owners do.

They take their rental income — every dollar of it — and deposit it directly onto their personal mortgage. This accelerates their mortgage paydown immediately.

Then they use a line of credit to pay their rental property expenses — maintenance, insurance, property tax, mortgage payments on the rental.

That line of credit is now being used for investment purposes. Which means the interest on that line of credit is fully tax deductible.

Every month — your personal mortgage gets smaller. Your tax-deductible debt gets larger. And at tax time — you get a refund instead of a bill.

I worked with a client recently — $450,000 mortgage, one rental property bringing in $1,400 a month. By restructuring exactly this way — they are on track to be mortgage-free 7 years sooner and will receive over $63,000 in tax refunds over the life of the strategy."

What If CRA Helped Pay Your Mortgage?

Script
"What if I told you that CRA — the Canada Revenue Agency — could actually help pay your mortgage every single year?

In Canada, when you borrow money for investment purposes — the interest on that borrowing is tax deductible. The government literally built this into the Income Tax Act to encourage investment.

When you use your mortgage strategically — redirecting how money flows between your mortgage, your investments, and your income — that mortgage interest can become fully tax deductible.

Completely legally. Completely CRA-approved. And generating a real tax refund every single year.

If you're in a 40% tax bracket and you're paying $25,000 a year in mortgage interest — making that interest tax deductible puts $10,000 back in your pocket every single year. That's $10,000 a year that CRA is essentially contributing to your mortgage.
